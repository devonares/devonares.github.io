What's Ikevamp?

It's short for Ikemen Vampire. It's one of Cybird's 'Ikemen Series' releases, their collection of Otome (dating simulation) games. This one game in particular has historical figures made into hot anime boys that you can date, and they're also vampires!

Why?

Uhhh.... I started playing this game semi-ironically, while I was bored in highschool. Of course, like most things, it did not stay ironic.

It combines my historical nerdery with my thrist for romantic stories very nicely, so in a while I grew obsessed, particularly with this one character, which is based on the real Napoleon Bonaparte.

Although he has his namesake, they really just made up his personality entirely (just like the other historical figures that also exists in this game.) You'll find that all these characters are actually pretty entertaining to see together.

Lore

Each of these characters, particularly characters on the middle and bottom row, are dead in their respective era but have asked to be revived again as a vampire. The one that revives them is the Lord of the Mansion, Comte de St Germain

St. Germain is a Greater Vampire. Greater basically means they are born a vampire and lives for eternity. It is almost impossible for them to get killed. They're also the only one with the ability to turn people into vampires.

The rest of the guys depicted here are Lesser vampires, meaning they were originally human but they've been turned, giving them longer life (quoted, "a taste of eternity") and stronger physique. They're harder to kill, but can still die.

The men on the top row are special, because they could be counted as the 'villains' of the game--except for Sebastian, who is a mortal human just living with us on the mansion.

Vlad, the white haired one, is another Greater Vampire. Greater vampires are basically sort of... archaic beings that have lived thousands of years. Some of them could time travel (Vlad and the Comte being one), making them wise to the world.

Vlad and the Comte stands on opposite sides, and each has a different ideology regarding humans and their civilization. But it could be argued that both ideas came from the same source: a love for them.

The Comte loves humanity, and he is willing to support, accomodate, and observe their achievments. Vlad too, but he has apparently seen the future where the world was going to be destroyed (remember his time traveling capabilities). He wanted to control humanity and direct them in the right way (his way.) These two sides are, to be honest, pretty lazy in continuing their conflict, because during most of the game they are pretty much just chilling.

The player, us, have accidentally stumbled into a time-traveling doorway and into this mansion located in 19th Century France. You're stuck for at least a month there, so you have time to get to know one of these boys, and perhaps fall in love.

On to Napoleon

He's the poster boy of the game, so, typically, he has an all around likeable and charismatic personality. Some would say he's boring and not interesting that way, but I disagree. Obviously.

While he is kind to you, he often went about it in an rude and awkward way. He likes to tease and chide you, as well as call you stupid (affectionate). He's also often overly serious, making him bad at understanding jokes made at his expense, AND having some really bad dad-quality jokes himself. This is making him the ultimate "that guy" of the game, in my opinion.

For the other characters, he's basically the older bro of the mansion. He is reliable, has leader-like qualities, *and* fighting capabilites. The coolest guy around.
